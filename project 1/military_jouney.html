Welcome to My Military Journey

Major Peter Morales, USMC

Major Morales is currently retired with over 30 years of service to the U.S. Marine Corps. He had ran the full gamut of a "Mustang" career in the military while on active duty and as a reservist, both as an enlisted member and as an Officer. His last call to duty was serving as the Marine Advisor/Liaison for the U.S. Navy Commodore of the 4th Naval Expeditionary Logistics Regiment (FOURTH NELR) in Jacksonville, Florida.

After graduating from high school in Queens, New York, Major Morales first enlisted in the United States Marine Corps in 1988, as a Field Radio Operator. He completed recruit training at MCRD Parris Island after his freshman year in college in 1989. He rose to the rank of Staff Sergeant, and served as a Platoon Sergeant and Radio Chief for infantry battalions. During that time, he provided communications support in Operation Desert Shield/Desert Storm, a counterdrug operation serving with Joint Task Force Six (JTF-6) near the Arizona/Mexico border, a multinational peacekeeping exercise Baltic Challenge in Lithuania, and conducted numerous Combined Arms Exercises (CAX) at Marine Corps Air-Ground Training Center (MCAGTC) 29 Palms, near Joshua Tree, California.

As a Sergeant, Morales also served at HQ, 1st Marine Corps (Recruiting) District from 1993 to 1998 as an Operations Clerk, then as a civilian government employee as a Supply Warehouseman and Purchasing agent. While a drilling Reservist in 1998, Staff Sergeant Morales completed Officer Candidate School in 1999, and graduated from the State University of New York at Stony Brook in 2000, with a multi-disciplinary Bachelor’s degree in Business, Psychology and Latin American & Caribbean Studies.

Upon his commissioning in June 2000, Lieutenant Morales transferred to Quantico, VA, where he worked at the Marine Corps University and facilitated the Advanced Logistics Officers Course. He was later selected to be a Logistics Officer upon completing The Basic School in June 2001.

Lieutenant Morales reported for duty with "The Untouchables" at Marine Wing Support Squadron (MWSS-272) in MCAS New River, NC, as a Motor Transport Officer in Oct 2001. Other billets held there include Assistant Operations Officer (S-3A), Maintenance Management Officer (MMO), and Headquarters Company Commander. He helped build and managed a 5,000 man tent city as Camp Commandant for Camp Snakepit in Ali Al Salem, Kuwait during Operation Iraqi Freedom in 2003. He was also dispatched to Marine Aviation Weapons and Training Squadron (MAWTS-1) as the Embark Officer to support the Weapons and Tactics Instructor Course in 2004.

Captain Morales transferred to 3rd Battalion, 3rd Marines ("America's Battalion") in Kaneohe Bay, HI, as the Battalion Director of Logistics (S-4) in June 2004. He arrived in time to embark equipment and personnel on amphibious ships and participate in the Rim of the Pacific Exercise (RIMPAC). He then coordinated logistics for 5 geographically separated locations, as the battalion conducted distributed operations throughout Eastern Afghanistan. Upon his return in 2005, he checked into the 3rd Marine Regiment to be their Motor T Officer and MMO, then quickly earning his role as Assistant Regimental Logistics Officer (Regt S-4A). It was there that he had completed the Expeditionary Warfare School Non-Resident Course. He was later hand-selected to join a Military Training Team (MTT) to train the 2-2-7 Iraqi Army Battalion staff and HQ Company in logistics and security operations in Haditha, Iraq in 2006.

From 2007 to 2011, Captain Morales served as the Executive Officer for Recruiting Station Harrisburg, overseeing officer and enlisted recruiting for Eastern and Central Pennsylvania, and being a proof source for diversity, effectively managed the high school program with a focus on community relations and the NROTC Scholarship.

In June 2011, Captain Morales transferred to the Individual Ready Reserve (IRR) and was later hired on an Individual Mobilized Augmentee (IMA) billet as a Logistics Planner at U.S. Marine Corps Forces, South (MARFORSOUTH), before receiving Active Duty-Operational Service (ADOS) orders in Sept 2012. Major Morales was promoted to his current rank in Oct 2012. Other billets held during his tenure included Sustainment Branch Head and using his cultural expertise, assisting the G-5 regional desk officers as the G-4 Theater Security Cooperation Branch Head until transferring back to the IRR in Aug 2015. It was here where, as a civilian Technical Support Engineer, he effectively communicated with customers and team members via phone regarding hardware, software, network and security related issues on behalf of Dell Corporation. Major Morales rejoined MARFORSOUTH serving as the G-4 Logistics Planner as part of the IMA detachment from June 2017 before receiving another set of ADOS orders until June 2018.

Major Morales has two daughters, Rosa (19) and Lisandra (18), which both had moved on to pursue collegiate pursuits after living with him in Tampa, Florida.

Major Morales’ personal awards include the Navy and Marine Corps Commendation Medal with 2 Gold Stars, the Navy and Marine Corps Achievement Medal with 3 Gold Stars, and the Combat Action Ribbon.
