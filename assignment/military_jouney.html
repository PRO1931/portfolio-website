Major Peter Morales, USMC

Major Morales is currently retired with over 30 years of service to the U.S. Marine Corps. His last call to duty was serving as the Marine Advisor/Liaison for the U.S. Navy Commodore of the 4th Naval Expeditionary Logistics Regiment (FOURTH NELR) in Jacksonville, Florida.

After graduating from high school in Queens, New York, Major Morales enlisted in the Marine Corps in 1988, as a Field Radio Operator. He completed recruit training at MCRD Parris Island after his freshman year in college in 1989. He rose to the rank of Staff Sergeant, and served as a Platoon Sergeant and Radio Chief for infantry battalions. During that time, he provided communications support in Operation Desert Shield/Desert Storm, a counterdrug operation serving JTF-6 near the Arizona/Mexico border, a multinational peacekeeping exercise Baltic Challenge in Lithuania, and conducted numerous Combined Arms Exercises (CAX) at MCAGTC 29 Palms.
